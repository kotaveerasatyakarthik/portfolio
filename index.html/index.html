Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman. Known as the “Missile Man of India,” he played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space and military missile development programs.

Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, earning the love and respect of millions. After his presidency, he dedicated his life to teaching, writing, and inspiring young minds until his passing in 2015.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam’s vision for India was of a developed and self-reliant nation. He launched initiatives such as the “What Can I Give Movement,” inspiring young people to contribute positively to society.

Numerous institutions, foundations, and awards have been named after him, honoring his contributions to science, leadership, and education.
